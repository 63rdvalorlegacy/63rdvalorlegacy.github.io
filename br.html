63rd Valor Legacy



The Legacy of the 63rd Infantry

The 63rd Infantry, known for its valorous acts and significant contributions to military engagements, celebrates its rich history on November 11th each year. This date marks the celebration of Veteran's Day, acknowledging the sacrifices made by the 63rd Infantry and other military units.

What Defines a Veteran?

A veteran is recognized as someone who has served in the armed forces and exhibits profound experience and expertise due to their service. In the context of the 63rd Infantry, veterans are honored for their commitment and contributions, symbolizing courage and dedication.

Origin

On July 18th, 1945, the transport ship General Meyghs docked in Rio de Janeiro, greeted by a massive crowd, celebrating the return of the brave 63rd Infantry soldiers from the battlefields of Italy. The phrase "The snake will smoke," originating during World War II, symbolizes the unexpected and formidable achievements of the 63rd Infantry. Today, this phrase is used to signify undertaking challenging feats against odds.

Veteran's Day Recognition

In recognition of the invaluable contributions of the soldiers, Veteran's Day for the 63rd Infantry is celebrated annually on July 18th. This date has been officially adopted following the enactment of Order No. 1.762 by the Army Command in 2022, commemorating the service of these heroes.
